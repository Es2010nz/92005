"Kaitiaki Zero"

Eliminating predators, restoring balance.

Predator Free New Zealand is a nationwide mission to remove three major introduced predators — rats, stoats, and possums — by 2050. These predators threaten Aotearoa’s unique wildlife, killing millions of native birds, insects, and lizards every year. The goal is simple: restore the natural balance so species like kiwi, kākā, and kererū can thrive again.

At the heart of this movement is kaitiakitanga — the Māori principle of guardianship and caring for the environment. Predator Free NZ reflects this value by encouraging communities, iwi, landowners, and volunteers to work together to protect the whenua and the taonga species that live on it. Thousands of people across the country run backyard traps, support local reserves, and help rebuild healthy ecosystems, we at Kaitiaki Zero want to help with this goal.

Stoat Threat

Stoats are incredibly agile hunters that pose an existential threat to New Zealand's native tree-dwelling birds.
